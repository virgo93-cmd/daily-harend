## The Science: How Retatrutide Works
Unlike its predecessors that targeted one or two receptors, Retatrutide is often referred to as a "triple agonist." It targets three specific receptors:
1. **GLP-1 (Glucagon-like peptide-1):** Regulates appetite and blood sugar.
2. **GIP (Glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide):** Enhances insulin secretion and further aids in metabolism.
3. **Glucagon:** Increases energy expenditure, effectively "burning" more calories.

This triple-action mechanism is why researchers and the medical community are keeping a close eye on clinical trial results. It is not just about suppressing hunger; it is about metabolic recalibration.
