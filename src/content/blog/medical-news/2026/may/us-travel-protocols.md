## Understanding the New Travel Mandates
In May 2026, U.S. authorities introduced updated guidelines directly impacting Green Card holders (Permanent Residents) traveling to and from regions in Africa currently affected by recent health alerts. For permanent residents, understanding these changes is crucial to ensuring your immigration status remains unaffected and your travel plans proceed smoothly.

## Key Points of the New Protocols
Based on the latest updates, here are the essential details you need to be aware of:
1. **Enhanced Screening:** Expect stricter health screening procedures at airports for anyone with a travel history from affected zones within the last 21 days.
2. **Additional Documentation:** Green Card holders are now strongly advised to carry proof of residency status and current medical records to expedite processing at border control.
3. **Travel Advisories:** The U.S. government has adjusted travel advisory levels for specific regions. Please be aware that this can impact your travel insurance coverage.

## Impact on Residency Status
The U.S. government emphasizes that these protocols are purely preventive measures for public health, not an attempt to revoke residency status. However, for Green Card holders, please note the following:
* **No Residency Cancellations:** These measures are strictly health-related and do not jeopardize your permanent resident status.
* **Compliance is Key:** Failure to adhere to quarantine instructions or health screenings could lead to significant entry delays or administrative penalties.

## Recommended Steps for Travelers
If you are planning to travel internationally soon:
* **Monitor Official Status:** Regularly check the official U.S. Department of State website for the latest travel advisories.
* **Prepare Your Documents:** Ensure your Green Card and supporting documentation are valid and easily accessible.
* **Medical Consultation:** If you feel exposed or develop symptoms, contact medical professionals before attempting to return to the U.S. to avoid complications at the border.
